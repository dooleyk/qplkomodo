Text Line Formatting

The characters, ", #, &, =, <, >, %, $, and @ have special meaning in HTML files. These characters will be automatically converted to codes, called "HTML entities," when used in question answer or text lines so that they will be displayed as intended and not used as HTML codes. All extended characters are also changed to HTML entities. You may prevent a character from being changed by preceding it with a backslash (\) character (i.e., a process called "escaping" a character).
